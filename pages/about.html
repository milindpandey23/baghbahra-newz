About Baghbahra

Baghbahra (also spelled as Bagbahara) is a town and a nagar panchayat in Mahasamund district in the state of Chhattisgarh, India. It is located approximately 90 kilometers from Raipur, the capital city of Chhattisgarh.

The town is situated on National Highway No. 353 and is well-connected by road and rail. Baghbahra has its own railway station on the Raipur-Mahasamund-Visakhapatnam rail route, making it easily accessible for visitors.

According to the 2011 Census of India, Baghbahra had a population of 14,337. The town has a literacy rate of 80.77%, higher than the national average of 74.04%.

The economy of Baghbahra is primarily agricultural, with rice being the main crop. The town serves as a market center for the surrounding rural areas, where farmers bring their produce for sale.

Baghbahra and its surrounding areas are home to several attractions, including the Chandi Mata Mandir in Ghunchapali village, Charoda Dam (Keshwa Reservoir), and other scenic spots. The region's natural beauty and cultural significance make it an interesting destination for visitors.

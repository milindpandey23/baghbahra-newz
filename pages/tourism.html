Tourism in Baghbahra and Nearby Areas

Baghbahra and the surrounding areas in Mahasamund district offer a variety of attractions for visitors, from religious sites to scenic dams and natural beauty spots. Explore the rich cultural heritage and picturesque landscapes that make this region special.

Chandi Mata Mandir

Location: Ghunchapali village, about 6-7 km from Bagbahara

The Chandi Mata Mandir (also known as Chandi Temple or Chandi Dongri Temple) is one of the most significant religious sites in the region. The temple features a natural mega idol of Devi Chandi and attracts thousands of devotees, especially during the Navratri festival.

The temple is particularly busy during Navarathri of Chaitra month when a special Mela (fair) is celebrated. Devotees from surrounding villages and towns visit to seek blessings from Goddess Chandi.

According to some sources, the temple is also known for bears that regularly visit the temple premises, earning it the nickname "Bear Temple" among some visitors.

How to Reach:

  • By Road: The temple is approximately 6-7 km from Bagbahara town
  • By Train: The nearest railhead is at Mahasamund
  • By Air: The nearest airport is at Raipur

Charoda Dam (Keshwa Reservoir Project)

Location: Near Bagbahara, Mahasamund district

Charoda Dam, also known as the Keshwa Reservoir Project, is a scenic water reservoir near Bagbahara. The dam offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape and is a peaceful spot for visitors to relax and enjoy nature.

The reservoir serves both practical purposes for irrigation and as a recreational spot for locals and tourists. The serene environment makes it an ideal location for picnics and nature photography.

Activities:

  • Picnicking
  • Nature viewing
  • Photography

Kodar Dam

Location: Mahasamund district, approximately 60 km from Raipur city

Kodar Dam is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Mahasamund district. This picturesque location offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and recreational activities, making it an ideal spot for travelers seeking tranquility and adventure.

As you approach Kodar Dam, you'll first notice the expansive view of the reservoir, with its calm waters reflecting the sky. The dam is an earthfill structure that efficiently manages water resources for the region's agriculture.

One of the highlights of visiting Kodar Dam is witnessing the sunset. As the sun dips below the horizon, the sky is painted with hues of orange and pink, creating a breathtaking spectacle that reflects off the water's surface.

Activities:

  • Boating (pedal boats: 50 rupees per boat, motorboats: 200 rupees per person)
  • Camping in open spaces around the dam
  • Picnicking
  • Photography
  • Nature walks

Entry Fee: 20 rupees per person for boating area (alternate route available with no fee)

Best Time to Visit: Winter season (October to February) for comfortable temperatures

Singhoda Dam

Location: Bagbahara Pithora Road, Bagbahra, Mahasamund district

Singhoda Dam is another water reservoir in the Bagbahara area that offers scenic views and a peaceful environment. The dam serves as an important water resource for the surrounding agricultural lands.

Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the area and spend time relaxing by the water. The dam is easily accessible from Bagbahara town.

Sirpur Archaeological Site

Location: Mahasamund district, Chhattisgarh

Sirpur is one of the famous places to visit in Mahasamund district and is known for its archaeological sites located at the banks of the Mahanadi River. The site offers a complete blend of culture and architectural beauty.

Sirpur is home to various ancient temples, monasteries, and stupas. One of the top places to visit is the Laxman Temple, which is solely dedicated to the Hindu God Lord Vishnu, who is believed to be the coordinator of the world according to Hindu Scriptures.

According to legend, the temple was built in the 7th century and showcases black architecture and intricate carvings that showcase the region's rich cultural heritage. In the older times, the city was known as Shripur and was the capital of South Kosal.

Another must-visit site is the Buddha Vihar of Sirpur, which is believed to be from the 8th century and gives a glimpse of the Buddhism that flourished here.

Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary

Location: Mahasamund district, Chhattisgarh

Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area in Mahasamund district that offers visitors a chance to experience the diverse wildlife and natural beauty of the region. The sanctuary is home to various species of animals and birds.

Visitors can enjoy wildlife viewing, nature photography, and the peaceful environment of the sanctuary. It's an ideal destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Baghbahra and the surrounding attractions is during the winter season (October to February) when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for outdoor activities.

The summer months (March to June) can be quite hot, with temperatures rising significantly, making outdoor exploration less comfortable.

The monsoon season (July to September) brings lush green landscapes but may limit accessibility to some areas due to heavy rainfall.

How to Reach Baghbahra

By Air: The nearest airport is at Raipur, which is approximately 90 kilometers from Baghbahra. From Raipur, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Baghbahra.

By Train: Baghbahra is situated on the Raipur-Mahasamund-Visakhapatnam rail route. The town has its own railway station, making it easily accessible by train.

By Road: Baghbahra is well-connected by road and is situated on National Highway No. 353. Regular bus services are available from Mahasamund, Raipur, and other nearby towns.
